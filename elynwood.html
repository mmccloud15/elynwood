About

Originally from New York City, Elizabeth Lynwood packed up and moved to Washington State at the age of eighteen. After attending college at the University of Washington, she worked as a designer and then brand manager for several luxury activewear brands. She spent her free time writing what would later be published as Before We Break Apart, before publishing the immediate best-seller Lark.

Currently, Elizabeth lives just outside of Seattle in a 100 year-old four-bedroom cabin that she renovated with her husband Eli. They live with one dog (Emmy), one cat (Eric), and one ghost that they have dubbed Earl. When she isn't writing or reading, Elizabeth can be found hiking with Eli and Emmy, searching for the best ramen in town, or visiting her favorite city, Vancouver.
